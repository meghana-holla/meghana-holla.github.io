About Me

Hello! I am a Graduate Student at Virginia Tech pursuing my Master of Science in Computer Science. My coursework mainly focuses on the areas of Machine Learning & Artifical Intelligence, Data & Information and Human Computer Interaction. I am part of Dr. Ismini Lourentzou’s research group and am working on Multimodal Machine Learning (ML) catering to language and vision, specifically, Natural Language Grounding in Videos.

I hold a Bachelor’s (B.Tech) degree in Computer Science and Engineering with specialization in Data Science from PES University, Bangalore, India. My undergraduate thesis titled "Dialog-Driven Face Generation usin GANs" falls in the area of conditional image generation. To elaborate, the work caters to the problem of generating a human face conditioned upon a natural language description. The contributions of the work were two-fold:

  1. A description-driven face generation module, which is capable of generating a face and also making corrections to the generated face through iterative natural language feedback. The multi-pass nature enables a communication with the generator model to be in the form of a dialog.
  2. An effective pipeline which includes a speech-to-text module for ergonomic interfacing, making interactions with the model more organic.

The work was accepted for publication at the 32nd IEEE International Conference on Tools with Artificial Intelligence (ICTAI 2020) held in Baltimore, MD (Acceptance rate: 25%)
